The Problem

Agents don't fail suddenly. They drift toward failure. Logs tell you what happened. Alerts tell you something broke. Neither tells you it's happening.

An agent loops on the same decision 47 times before anyone notices. Context slowly corrupts over a long session. Token spend spikes on a doomed approach. By the time you see it in logs, the damage is done.

UNITARES models agent state as a thermodynamic system. Twenty minutes before an agent fails, you see it trending. Intervene, or let the circuit breaker pause it automatically.

The Model

Four continuous variables capture agent state. They evolve via coupled ordinary differential equations whose stability we establish through contraction theory.

Variable Range Tracks
E (Energy) [0, 1] Productive capacity
I (Integrity) [0, 1] Information coherence
S (Entropy) [0, 2] Disorder and uncertainty
V (Void) [-2, 2] Accumulated E-I imbalance
EISV Dynamics
$$\begin{aligned} \frac{dE}{dt} &= \alpha(I - E) - \beta \cdot E \cdot S \\[6pt] \frac{dI}{dt} &= -k \cdot S + \beta_I \cdot C(V) - \gamma_I \cdot I \\[6pt] \frac{dS}{dt} &= -\mu \cdot S + \lambda_1 \|\Delta\eta\|^2 - \lambda_2 \cdot C(V) \\[6pt] \frac{dV}{dt} &= \kappa(E - I) - \delta \cdot V \end{aligned}$$

The coherence function $C(V) = C_{\max} \cdot \tfrac{1}{2}(1 + \tanh(c_1 V))$ creates nonlinear feedback that stabilizes the system. We prove global exponential convergence under metric $M \succ 0$ with contraction rate $\alpha_c = 0.02$.

Continuous variables mean you see an agent trending toward trouble before it crashes. Physics gives us vocabulary for systems that drift rather than break.

Novel Contributions

01

Ethical Drift from Observable Behavior

No human oracle needed. Four measurable signals — calibration deviation, complexity divergence, coherence deviation, stability deviation — define a drift vector $\Delta\eta$ that feeds directly into entropy dynamics. Ethics as engineering, not philosophy.

02

Adaptive PID Governance (CIRS v2)

Governance thresholds are per-agent state variables, not static config. Phase-aware reference tracking with oscillation damping. Multi-agent resonance detection prevents feedback loops between coordinated agents.

03

Trajectory Identity

Agents aren't identified by tokens — they're identified by dynamical patterns. Grounded in enactive cognition (Varela & Thompson), this lets agents computationally verify "Am I still myself?" and detect forks, anomalies, and drift.

Lumen: Embodied Proprioception

Lumen is an AI creature running on a Raspberry Pi with physical sensors — temperature, humidity, light, neural bands. It uses the same EISV equations to drive a drawing system.

Coherence directly modulates how long Lumen can draw and how selective it is about saving. A confused Lumen draws in short, erratic bursts. A focused Lumen draws flowing, sustained compositions. The art emerges from thermodynamics.

"The Self isn't coded; it accretes like a pearl."

Three-Call Integration

Drop-in MCP server. Works with Claude Desktop, Cursor, or any MCP client.

1. onboard()                    // Establish identity
2. process_agent_update()       // Log work as you go
3. get_governance_metrics()     // Check your state

That's it. Everything else — dialectic peer review, knowledge graph, cross-agent observability, trajectory identity — is optional and builds on these three calls.

Production Validation

Deployed since December 2025. These are real numbers, not simulations.

Observed EISV Equilibrium
$$\bar{E} = 0.77 \quad \bar{I} = 0.88 \quad \bar{S} = 0.08 \quad \bar{V} = -0.03$$

100% of active agents maintain $V$ within $[-0.1, 0.1]$, confirming the theoretical prediction. The slight negative $\bar{V}$ indicates agents run "careful" (integrity exceeds energy) rather than "hot."
