# UNITARES Pitch — Investor Meeting Checklist

## Pre-Meeting Preparation

### Materials Ready
- [ ] Pitch deck (PDF + presentation format)
- [ ] One-pager executive summary (PDF)
- [ ] Dashboard demo (live or recorded video)
- [ ] Q&A document (for reference)
- [ ] Quick reference card (print out)
- [ ] Business card / contact info

### Content Mastery
- [ ] Memorized elevator pitch (30 seconds)
- [ ] Know all key numbers (600+ agents, 46 tools, etc.)
- [ ] Can explain thermodynamic model clearly
- [ ] Understand competitive landscape
- [ ] Know business model inside and out
- [ ] Have use of funds breakdown ready
- [ ] Know your ask amount and why

### Technical Setup
- [ ] Dashboard accessible (local or ngrok)
- [ ] Demo video ready (if live demo fails)
- [ ] Laptop charged
- [ ] Backup presentation (USB drive)
- [ ] Internet connection tested
- [ ] Screen sharing works

### Research
- [ ] Researched the investor/firm
- [ ] Know their portfolio companies
- [ ] Understand their investment thesis
- [ ] Know their typical check size
- [ ] Found mutual connections (if any)
- [ ] Read their blog posts/articles

---

## Day of Meeting

### 1 Hour Before
- [ ] Review quick reference card
- [ ] Practice elevator pitch out loud
- [ ] Review Q&A document
- [ ] Check dashboard is working
- [ ] Test screen sharing
- [ ] Review investor's background
- [ ] Prepare 3-5 questions to ask them

### 15 Minutes Before
- [ ] Arrive early (if in-person)
- [ ] Set up laptop (if needed)
- [ ] Test internet connection
- [ ] Have materials ready
- [ ] Take a deep breath
- [ ] Review opening line

### During Meeting
- [ ] Start with problem (make it visceral)
- [ ] Show demo early (prove it works)
- [ ] Use Gemini quote (it's powerful)
- [ ] Emphasize "working system"
- [ ] Address competition clearly
- [ ] Listen actively to questions
- [ ] Answer directly, don't ramble
- [ ] Use power phrases naturally
- [ ] End with vision, not ask
- [ ] Ask for next step

### After Meeting
- [ ] Send thank you email within 24 hours
- [ ] Include one-pager PDF
- [ ] Reference specific conversation points
- [ ] Offer to answer follow-up questions
- [ ] Propose concrete next step
- [ ] Follow up if no response in 1 week

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- [ ] Don't get too technical
- [ ] Don't bash competitors
- [ ] Don't oversell — let traction speak
- [ ] Don't rush — pause for impact
- [ ] Don't read slides — talk to audience
- [ ] Don't ignore questions — address them
- [ ] Don't be defensive — be confident
- [ ] Don't forget to ask for next step
- [ ] Don't forget to listen — it's a conversation
- [ ] Don't forget to follow up

## Red Flags to Watch For

**If they say:**
- "Can you build this as a feature?" → They don't get infrastructure play
- "This seems like research" → They don't see business model
- "What if agents don't want this?" → They haven't seen traction
- "Why not just use [competitor]?" → They don't see differentiation

**Your response:**
- Stay calm
- Address directly
- Use data (600+ agents)
- Reinforce positioning
- Don't get defensive

## Success Metrics

**Good signs:**
- They ask about team/background
- They want to see demo
- They introduce you to others
- They ask about timeline
- They reference your pitch later

**Warning signs:**
- They don't ask questions
- They seem distracted
- They cut meeting short
- They don't engage with demo
- They're overly skeptical

**If warning signs:**
- Don't panic
- Stay confident
- Address concerns directly
- Ask what would help
- Follow up anyway
